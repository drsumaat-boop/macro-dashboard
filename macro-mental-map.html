The Trunk — Master Root
🌳 Interest Rates = Direction of All Money
The Fed controls the price of money. That price determines where every dollar in the global economy flows. Everything else is downstream of this single fact.
▼
STEP 1 — THE FRAME
🏛 Fed Policy
Where is the rate cycle? This sets gravity for all assets.
▼
Fed Tools
▼
Fed Funds Rate — overnight rate, the starting price of all money
Forward Guidance — words move markets before action. Bond market prices this immediately
QE / QT — buying or selling bonds. QE adds money, QT removes it
Dot Plot — Fed's own forecast of future rates. Compare to bond market pricing
Rate Direction → Asset Flow
▼
Rates FALLING ↓ — money flows into stocks, gold, real estate, EM, commodities. Growth assets appreciate
Rates RISING ↑ — money flows into cash, short bonds, financials, dollar. Growth assets punished
Direction = Gravity — high rates = strong gravity pulling money to safe yield. Low rates = money floats into risk
Anticipation > Action — markets move on EXPECTATION of rate change, not the change itself. Be early
Where to Look
▼
FRED: FEDFUNDS — actual Fed Funds rate today
CME FedWatch — market probability of next hike/cut. Most forward looking tool
FRED: DGS2 — 2Y yield = bond market's real time verdict on Fed path
Golden Rule
→ Fed sets short end. Bond market sets long end. Both together = full picture
→ When Fed and bond market disagree — always side with bond market
→ 2Y yield = jury verdict. Fed announcement = judge reading it out. Jury decided weeks ago
THE GLUE — CONNECTS EVERYTHING
⚖️ Spot Bond Market
The master scoreboard. Largest, smartest money on the planet pricing all macro in real time.
▼
Price vs Yield — The Foundation
▼
Always inverse — price UP = yield DOWN. Price DOWN = yield UP. Never forget this
Fixed coupon — bond pays fixed amount. Pay more for it = lower effective return = lower yield
Selling bonds — means prices fall = yields rise = tighter conditions for everyone
🎯 Yield Curve — Most Important Single Number
▼
Steep (+0.5% or more) — early/mid expansion. Risk on. Favour cyclicals and growth
Flat (0 to +0.5%) — late cycle warning. Rotate to value and defensives
Inverted (negative) — recession signal. Capital preservation. Bonds and gold
Un-inverting from inversion — MOST DANGEROUS moment. Recession typically begins here not during inversion
FRED: T10Y2Y — 10Y minus 2Y. Most watched spread
FRED: T10Y3M — 10Y minus 3M. Fed's preferred measure. Often leads T10Y2Y
Curve Nuances — 7 Layers Deep
▼
Bull Steepening — both yields fall, short end faster. Fed cutting. MOST BULLISH for risk assets. Early expansion
Bear Flattening — both yields rise, short end faster. Fed hiking aggressively. Classic late cycle. Growth stocks hurt
Bear Steepening — both rise, long end faster. Inflation fears. Negative for bonds AND growth
Inversion Sequence — short end inverts first (2Y-5Y), then mid (5Y-10Y), then full (2Y-10Y). Earlier spreads give advance warning
Duration matters — weeks = technical noise. Months = genuine signal. Deeper = more severe
Global curves — US only = local issue. All major curves inverting = global recession signal. Much more serious
Real Yield (DFII10) — True Cost of Money
▼
Formula — Real Yield = Nominal Yield minus Inflation. Always check both
Negative real yield — most powerful tailwind for gold, real estate, commodities simultaneously
Rising real yield — gold falls. Growth stocks punished. Bonds attractive
Passive tightening — Fed holds rates but inflation falls = real yield rises = conditions tighten without Fed moving
FRED: DFII10, DFII5 — real yields at 10Y and 5Y maturities
Bond Market Leads Everything
▼
Sequence always — Bond market moves first → stocks follow weeks later → economy follows months later → Fed reacts last
January 2022 proof — 10Y rose January. S&P peaked January 3rd. Fed only hiked March. Bond market was months ahead
2Y = Jury verdict — 2Y above Fed rate = market pricing hikes. 2Y below Fed rate = market pricing cuts. Real time signal
STEP 2 — CORNER PIECES
🔥 Inflation
Drives Fed decisions. Rising = Fed forced to hike. Falling = Fed can cut.
▼
Key Readings
▼
FRED: CPIAUCSL — Consumer Price Index. Headline inflation monthly
FRED: T10YIE — 10Y Breakeven Inflation. What bond market EXPECTS inflation to be. Forward looking
Oil price — leads CPI by 2-3 months. Oil spike = inflation coming. Oil collapse = deflation risk
Rising inflation — Fed must hike. Bad for bonds. Bad for growth stocks. Good for commodities and value
Falling inflation — Fed can cut. Good for bonds. Good for growth stocks. Good for gold if real yields fall
STEP 3 — BACKGROUND SECTIONS
👷 Employment
Confirms real economy health. Strong jobs = Fed stays hawkish. Cracking = Fed pivots.
▼
Key Readings
▼
FRED: UNRATE — Unemployment rate. Rising = economy weakening = Fed will pivot
FRED: PAYEMS — Nonfarm payrolls. Monthly. Strong number = economy healthy
Initial jobless claims — weekly. First to show cracks in labor market. Watch weekly
Sahm Rule — when unemployment rises 0.5% from 12-month low = recession has started. Historically accurate
STEP 4 — CONNECTING PIECES
🔗 Credit Markets
The early warning system. Stress shows here before equity markets react.
▼
Credit Spread Readings
▼
Below 3.5% — tight spreads. Risk on. Confidence high. Favour equities and cyclicals
3.5% to 5% — stress building. Be selective. Reduce high beta exposure
Above 5% — risk off. Capital preservation. Bonds and gold
Above 500bps — move from stress to distress. Major risk-off event. Reduce everything
FRED: BAMLH0A0HYM2 — ICE BofA HY OAS. The key credit spread to watch weekly
STEP 5 — DETAIL PIECES
🔶 Growth Expectations
Real world demand confirmation. Copper = doctor of the economy.
▼
Copper/Gold Ratio — Best Single Growth Indicator
▼
Ratio rising — copper outperforming gold. Growth optimism. Industry buying copper. Risk on confirmed
Ratio falling — gold outperforming copper. Fear dominant. Slowdown signal. Risk off building
TradingView — CAPITALCOM:COPPERGOLD. Check weekly alongside yield curve
FRED: INDPRO — Industrial production. Confirms real demand in the economy
STEP 6 — THE BORDER
💵 US Dollar (DXY)
Transmission mechanism for global capital flows. Direction matters more than level.
▼
Dollar Direction → Asset Impact
▼
Strong dollar ↑ — commodities fall (priced in USD). EM suffers (dollar debt expensive). Gold headwind
Weak dollar ↓ — commodities rise. EM rallies. Gold tailwind. Risk assets globally supported
Dollar follows yields — higher US yields attract foreign capital = dollar strengthens. Lower yields = weakens
FRED: DTWEXBGS — USD Broad Index. More comprehensive than DXY alone
STEP 7 — FINAL PIECES
🛡 VIX + Gold — Risk Confirmation
Final check. Are investors behaving consistently with what the macro says?
▼
VIX Readings
▼
Below 15 — complacency. Late cycle warning. Market not pricing any risk
15 to 25 — normal healthy range. Risk appetite intact
25 to 35 — fear rising. Reduce risk. Be selective
Above 35-40 — panic. Potential buying opportunity if macro supports recovery
Gold Signals
▼
Gold rising + news bearish — MOST BULLISH signal. Smart money accumulating. Believe price not headlines
Gold vs real yield — strongest relationship in macro. Real yield falls = gold rises. Always check DFII10 with gold
Gold + copper both rising — growth optimism. Copper confirming gold's move is not just fear
Gold rising alone — pure fear signal. Dollar weakness or credit stress. Check VIX and credit spreads
THE OUTPUT — WHERE ALL 7 STEPS LEAD
🔄 Business Cycle Phase
All 7 inputs combined tell you which phase you are in. Phase tells you what to own.
▼
Phase 1 — Early Expansion
▼
Signals — curve steepening, spreads tightening, VIX falling, Fed cutting
Favour — cyclicals, growth stocks, small caps, EM, corporate bonds
Avoid — defensives, cash, long USD positions
Phase 2 — Mid Expansion
▼
Signals — curve positive, spreads tight, VIX low, earnings growing
Favour — broad equities, commodities rising, EM, real assets
Avoid — long bonds struggling, defensives underperform
Phase 3 — Late Expansion
▼
Signals — curve flattening, inflation rising, Fed hiking, spreads bottoming
Favour — commodities, value stocks, financials, energy, short duration
Avoid — growth/tech, long bonds, housing
Phase 4 — Contraction
▼
Signals — curve inverted then un-inverting, spreads widening, VIX spiking, earnings falling
Favour — government bonds, gold, USD, defensives (utilities, healthcare)
Avoid — cyclicals, commodities, EM, high yield bonds
